## What It Will Do

### Your 3D Self — The First Impression That Sticks
The moment someone arrives, they're greeted by a realistic 3D animated version of you. It moves, reacts, and feels alive on screen. This isn't a photo — it's an experience. No other candidate they're looking at has this. It immediately signals that you're someone who thinks differently and builds things that stand out.

### Your Skills — Shown, Not Just Listed
Your core strengths in JavaScript/TypeScript, React/Next.js, and databases (both SQL and NoSQL) are displayed in a way that's visual and striking — not just a boring bullet list. Each skill feels weighted and intentional, like a showcase, not a résumé line. Visitors immediately understand what you're capable of and what kind of work they can hire you for.

### Your Projects — A Window Into Your Real Work
Each project you've built — CashBack Motors, the EVE robot, the Alexandria Barber Booking app, and more — gets its own spotlight card. Visitors can see what the project does, what you built, and go directly to the live version or the code. It turns your GitHub from a collection of files into a gallery of achievements.

### Your Certificates & Education — Your Credentials, Beautifully Presented
You're a Computer Engineering student at AASTMT, active in the AWS Community Core Team, and you've earned real certifications. These are displayed with weight and clarity — not buried in a PDF. When a client or employer sees your academic background and credentials, they feel confident they're talking to someone serious.

### Your Living Update System — The Site That Grows With You
You have a private area of the site only you can access. From there, you can add new projects, new skills, or new certificates whenever you're ready. On top of that, the site is connected to your GitHub — so any project you pin there automatically shows up on your portfolio without you having to do anything extra. The site never goes stale.

### Your Contact Zone — Making It Easy to Reach You
At the bottom of the site, visitors find everything they need — your email, your Egyptian and Saudi phone numbers, and clean links to your LinkedIn, GitHub, Instagram, X, and Facebook. There's a simple message form so people can reach out without needing to open their email app. Whether someone wants to hire you full-time or ask about a freelance project, the path from "interested" to "in your inbox" is frictionless.
